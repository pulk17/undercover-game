Core Game Mechanics

Undercover is a social deduction game where hidden identities meet linguistic warfare. The core tension is elegant: everyone must say enough to prove they belong, but not so much that they give away which word they actually know. Every sentence is a gamble.


The Fundamental Premise

A host picks a secret word pair — two words that are related but distinct. Civilians all receive Word A. The Undercover player receives Word B. Mr. White receives nothing. Each round, players give one-word or one-phrase clues about their word without saying it directly. After clues, the group votes to eliminate whoever they suspect is not one of them.


Word Pair Logic

Word pairs must be close enough that an Undercover player can convincingly bluff, yet distinct enough that careful listeners catch inconsistencies.

Coffee vs Tea
Swimming Pool vs Lake
Hospital vs Clinic

The Clue Balance Problem

Every player faces the same impossible tension each round, just from different angles.

Too Vague → Voted Out
Sounds like you don't know the word. Other civilians may think you're the spy.
Too Specific → Helps the Undercover
Giving a highly specific clue gives the Undercover player exactly the information they need to blend in.
Just Right → Social Proof
A clue that proves you know your word without telegraphing it to outsiders.

Win Conditions

Civilians Win

All Undercover players and Mr. White are eliminated. At least two civilians must remain at game end.

Undercover Wins

Number of Undercoverts equals or exceeds the number of remaining civilians at any vote phase.

Mr. White Wins

Survives until only 3 players remain, OR correctly guesses the civilian word when eliminated.

Key Tactical Dynamics

Civilian Tactics

  • Give clues that only someone with Word A would know — use specific, niche attributes
  • Listen for players whose clues are slightly off — they reveal the undercover word, not the civilian one
  • Coordinate in discussion: "Your clue of 'bitter' works for both — say something more specific"
  • Don't go first — later clues let you calibrate specificity after hearing others
  • Avoid buzzword clues shared by both words (e.g., "drink" for coffee/tea)
  • Protect Mr. White accidentally — don't let them pick up too much info from your clues
  • Watch clue ordering — if two people gave the same clue in different rounds, one is copying

Undercover Tactics

  • Listen to the first 2-3 clues before deciding your strategy — derive patterns from civilian clues
  • Mirror civilian clue themes at the right abstraction level — match the vibe, not the word
  • Be the most vocal accuser — shift suspicion before the vote
  • Target players who are closest to outing you — eliminate your best detectives first
  • If your word is "Tea", and civilians say "espresso" and "foam", give a clue like "ritual" — works for both
  • Manufacture doubt: "That clue was weird — could mean either word"
  • In final 3, pivot hard: convincingly name the civilian word to win as Mr. White

Player Roles

Civilian

The Majority

Receives the main (correct) word. Their job is to prove they know it without revealing it to the Undercover. They must also correctly identify and eliminate all Undercoverts and Mr. White before being voted out themselves.

  • Sees: Word A (the main word)
  • Majority of players per game
  • Must distinguish between Word A and Word B nuance
  • Must protect the word from Mr. White's deduction
Undercover

The Impostor

Receives a related but different word (Word B). Must blend into civilian discussions by giving plausible clues, all while deducing what the civilian word really is. The master of misdirection.

  • Sees: Word B (the related word)
  • 1–2 players per game depending on mode
  • Knows they are the Undercover from the start
  • Can optionally know other Undercoverts (mode-dependent)
Mr. White

The Blank

Receives nothing — no word at all. The hardest role. Must listen, deduce, and construct believable clues from zero information. If eliminated, may guess the civilian word to steal the win.

  • Sees: Nothing — a blank card
  • Optional role: 0–1 per game
  • Only player who can win by guessing the word
  • Ultimate high-risk, high-reward role

Special Ability: Detective (Premium Role)

Optional Premium

One civilian is secretly designated as the Detective. They gain one private accusation per game — they can send a hidden accusation to the host app (or all players in a reveal moment). If correct, their team gains bonus points. If wrong, the Detective is immediately eliminated.

  • Only usable once, before the public vote
  • If correct — accused player is eliminated without a vote
  • If wrong — Detective is eliminated immediately
  • Other players do not know who the Detective is

Mr. White's Guess Mechanics

When Mr. White is eliminated by vote, before their role is revealed, they enter a 10-second Guess Window where they type or speak the word they believe the civilians have.

  • Guess must be entered secretly — other players look away
  • Exact match OR close match (configurable) earns Mr. White a win
  • Failed guess: role is revealed, civilians win
  • Mr. White can also attempt guess at final 3 without being eliminated first
  • Close Match mode: synonyms accepted (e.g., "auto" for "car")

Game Modes

All modes are built on the same role + word system, but each changes the tension, pacing, information flow, or win condition in a meaningful way.


🎯
Free 2–12 Players

Classic Mode

The original formula. One Undercover, one optional Mr. White. One clue per player per round, then a vote. Play until one side wins or choose a fixed number of rounds. The purest expression of the game.

  • Configurable: with or without Mr. White
  • Configurable clue timer: 15s / 30s / 60s / unlimited
  • Customizable starting player (clockwise or random)
  • Post-elimination role reveal: immediate or end-of-game
⚡
Free 4–12 Players

Speed Round

A 20-second hard timer per clue. If a player doesn't give a clue in time, they automatically receive 1 strike. Three strikes in a game = eliminated. Forces intuition over deliberation — fast chaos, fast reveals.

  • 20s per clue (configurable: 10s / 20s / 30s)
  • Visible countdown on every device simultaneously
  • Skip penalty: 1 strike per skip
  • 3 strikes = forced elimination, role revealed
  • Discussion phase is also limited: 90s total group discussion
👥
Premium 6–12 Players

Team Mode

Players split into two teams (Red vs Blue). Each team has its own Undercover — Red team's spy is on the Blue team and vice versa. Teams discuss internally, then give clues alternating. First team to eliminate both enemy spies wins.

  • Teams can communicate privately during team phases
  • Each team has its own word pair — the spies have the opposite team's word
  • Team strategy chat: emoji-only during opponent clue turns
  • Bonus win: if your Undercover is never eliminated and your team wins
🌐
Premium 5–10 Players

Secret Alliance

Two Undercover players are assigned. They both know each other from the start (a private notification). They must coordinate without appearing to communicate. If both survive to final 4, they both win, even if civilians are majority.

  • Undercoverts see a subtle icon indicating the other's identity
  • Cannot directly communicate — must use clue patterns as signals
  • Civilians must identify BOTH Undercoverts to win
  • If one Undercover is eliminated, the other still plays solo
🎰
Premium 4–8 Players

Double Agent

Two Undercover players exist but each has a different related word. They don't know each other's identity. Now there are three word factions. Civilians must figure out there are two spies, find both, and eliminate them. The two Undercoverts are inadvertently competing against each other too.

  • Words: Civilians = A, Undercover 1 = B, Undercover 2 = C
  • Example: Apple / Pear / Orange — three word clusters in one room
  • Undercoverts win only if BOTH survive; partial survival = civilian win
  • Mr. White is disabled in this mode
🔄
Premium 4–10 Players

Reverse Mode

The roles and mechanics are flipped. Undercoverts now form the majority and the Civilians are the minority. Civilians must blend into the Undercover's world. This creates a completely different psychological dynamic — paranoia is reversed.

  • Civilians = 1–2 players. Undercoverts = majority
  • Undercoverts win by eliminating all civilians
  • Civilians win by surviving through all rounds or being the last one standing
  • Discussion strategies are completely reversed
👻
Premium 5–12 Players

Mr. White Army

No Undercover. Instead, 30–40% of players are Mr. White. They must all deduce the civilian word from clues and coordinate a collective guess. Each Mr. White proposes their guess during their clue turn. If majority match, they win instantly.

  • Civilians must vote out Mr. Whites without giving away the word
  • Mr. Whites secretly vote on a shared guess each round
  • If 2+ Mr. Whites give the same word in clue phase → instant collective guess trigger
  • Civilian win: all Mr. Whites eliminated before group guess
🏆
Free 2–12 Players

Tournament Mode

A multi-game bracket where players accumulate points across 5 games. Points are awarded for survival, correct eliminations, successful bluffs, and Mr. White correct guesses. Leaderboard shown after each game. Grand winner crowned at end.

  • 5 games per tournament, roles rotated automatically
  • Points: Survive a round (+1), Correct vote (+2), Win as Undercover (+5), Win as Mr. White (+8)
  • Persistent lobby — no one leaves between games
  • Roles distributed so everyone plays Undercover at least once

Word System & Categories

The word database is the heart of the game's replayability. Word pairs must be curated with care — too similar and the Undercover trivially blends in; too different and they are immediately exposed. The system must also scale across age groups, cultures, and languages.


Difficulty Tiers

Easy

Word pairs are well-known, concretely different, easily describable. Ideal for new players, families, or younger audiences. Overlap between words is high but the distinction is clear.

Dog vs Cat
Medium

Words share attributes but exist in distinct experiential zones. Requires players to think carefully about what is specific to their word versus shared properties.

Espresso vs Cappuccino
Hard

High overlap, subtle differences. Undercover players can convincingly bluff for many rounds. Civilians must listen with extreme precision. Best for experienced groups.

Accountant vs Auditor

Custom Word Mode

Players can create their own word pairs before a game. The host enters a word pair in the lobby setup screen. Custom words are not validated against difficulty — it's the players' responsibility to create fair pairs.

  • Host enters Word A and Word B before the game starts
  • Words are hidden from all players during entry
  • Custom pairs can be saved to a personal library
  • Community submission: submit pairs for app curation review
  • Custom Pack: group of 10+ custom pairs saved for recurring play

Word Database Technical Design

  • Minimum 2,000 word pairs at launch across all categories
  • Words tagged with: language, region, age-group, difficulty, category
  • Anti-repeat logic: system tracks used pairs per session, avoids repeats
  • Localization: Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, and 6+ other languages in Year 1
  • Community voting on submitted word pairs (approve/flag)
  • A/B tested difficulty ratings — adjust based on Undercover win rate per pair
  • Banned word list: culturally insensitive words filtered automatically

Phase 1 — Lobby & Setup

  • Host selects: Game Mode, Category, Difficulty, Timer Settings, Player Count
  • Players join via Room Code (6-digit alphanumeric, e.g. WOLF42)
  • Host can customize player nicknames or use their profiles
  • Host optionally enables Mr. White, Detective role
  • Players see a waiting room screen with animated player cards joining
  • Host can kick players or transfer host status
  • "Ready" button — game starts when all players tap ready

Phase 2 — Role Reveal (Private)

  • Each player holds their device face-down until told to look
  • App: "Everyone tap when ready to see your word — don't show others"
  • Player taps → word displays for 5 seconds, then auto-hides
  • Player can tap "Peek" to view word again (hidden tap, no log)
  • Mr. White sees: a blank envelope animation with "?" inside
  • Undercover sees: their word + subtle "UNDERCOVER" badge in red
  • Detective: sees their civilian word + detective badge (gold)
  • Once all players confirm, host starts the game

Phase 3 — Clue Round Rules

  • Play order shown on all screens — turn highlighted for active player
  • Active player must give exactly ONE clue (word, phrase, or gesture description)
  • Clue can be: a single word, a short phrase, a comparison, a metaphor
  • Clue CANNOT be: the word itself, part of the word, a direct synonym
  • Other players cannot react audibly until clue phase ends
  • Timer runs per clue (or unlimited if disabled)
  • All clues are logged in the app's game log for the round
  • Players who skip without cause receive a penalty flag

Phase 4 — Discussion

  • Open, free-form discussion among all surviving players
  • Duration: configurable — 60s / 90s / 120s / unlimited
  • All clues visible in a scrollable log on every device
  • Players may ask each other clarifying questions about their clues
  • No new clues allowed — discussion only
  • Host can extend time by 30 seconds if needed
  • Visual countdown timer shown to all players

Phase 5 — Voting

  • Each player sees a list of all surviving players (excluding themselves)
  • Players tap to vote — choices are submitted simultaneously
  • 30-second voting window — abstain = no vote cast (no penalty)
  • Votes hidden until all are submitted or time expires
  • Results reveal with animated vote count per player
  • Tie resolution: revote between tied players only, or random elimination (configurable)
  • Immunity: players with most prior eliminations cannot be voted against in this round (optional rule)

Phase 6 — Elimination & Win Check

  • Player with most votes is eliminated — dramatic reveal animation
  • Role revealed: Civilian / Undercover / Mr. White
  • If Mr. White: 10-second guess window opens on their device
  • Win condition checked: if met, game ends with full role reveal
  • If no win: new round begins (back to Phase 3)
  • Eliminated players can spectate subsequent rounds
  • Spectators see all roles — can observe but not communicate

Special Rules & Edge Cases

Tie Vote: When two or more players are tied for elimination votes, the app offers three resolution options chosen by the host before game start: (1) Re-vote between tied players only, (2) Tied players all survive this round, (3) Random elimination among tied players. Default is option 1.
Undercover Self-Reveal: An Undercover player may voluntarily reveal themselves at any point before voting. If they do, they have 15 seconds to name the civilian word correctly. Correct = Undercover wins immediately. Wrong = eliminated with no reveal of word (civilians don't learn the word from this).
Final 3 Rule: When only 3 players remain, the game enters Final Confrontation mode. Mr. White (if alive) gets a final guess attempt before voting. After the vote, regardless of outcome, all roles are revealed and the win condition check occurs.
Silent Round: An optional round type (host-enabled) where no clue is given — players only observe body language and must vote based on previous rounds. Adds a theatrical, pressure-cooker element for in-person play.

UI Design Language

Visual Identity

  • Dark-first UI — primary mode is dark background (near black)
  • Accent color: Amber/Gold (#E8C547) — used sparingly for key CTAs and highlights
  • Role colors: Blue (Civilian), Red (Undercover), Purple (Mr. White)
  • Typeface: Display — Syne or similar geometric bold for headings; Sans-serif for UI
  • Monospace font for codes, timers, and role labels (IBM Plex Mono)
  • Card-based layout for player slots, clue logs, and settings panels
  • Microanimations: role reveal flip card, vote count increment, elimination particle burst

UX Principles

  • One primary action per screen — no decision paralysis in game screens
  • Private screens: shake to dismiss, fingerprint-gated "peek" for word recall
  • Accessibility: large tap targets (44px min), high contrast modes, text size scaling
  • Vibration feedback on your turn notification
  • Portrait-only during gameplay; landscape allowed in menus
  • No app navigation during active game phase — modal-only interactions
  • Offline-safe: game state cached locally in case of connection drop

Multiplayer Architecture

🌐

Online Multiplayer

Players on different devices in different locations join the same game room. Real-time state synchronization via WebSockets. All game events (clues, votes, eliminations) pushed to all clients instantly.

📱

Local (Pass-and-Play)

One device, multiple players sitting together. Each player takes the phone for their private role reveal. All other game phases shown on-screen for everyone. No internet required after initial word load.

🔗

Hybrid Mode

Host device runs the game, other players optionally join via their own phones as secondary displays. Remote players see the same game state in real time but use the host for game authority.

Room System

Room Creation & Codes

  • Room Code: 6-character alphanumeric, e.g. WOLF42 — readable, no ambiguous chars (0/O, 1/I)
  • QR Code: generated per room for quick local join (scan = instant join)
  • Room link: shareable deep link undercoverapp.io/join/WOLF42
  • Room expiry: 24 hours after last activity
  • Private rooms: password-protected (optional)
  • Public rooms: matchmaking queue (join any open game)
  • Room capacity: 2–12 players (hard cap)

Host Controls

  • Kick player from room (with reason: "inactive", "wrong room")
  • Transfer host role to another player mid-lobby
  • Mute clue timer (per player, if they're experiencing issues)
  • Pause game (emergency pause visible to all)
  • Extend discussion time (+30s button)
  • Force-start when all players marked "ready"
  • Reset game: keep same players, draw new roles and words
  • End game early (host override — shown as host-ended, not a win/loss)

Connection & Resilience

Reconnect Flow

  • Player disconnects mid-game → shown as "Away" on all screens
  • 60-second reconnect window — game continues if others are present
  • On reconnect, player receives full current game state
  • If word was already assigned, it is restored securely (encrypted in session)
  • Host disconnect: auto-transfer to longest-connected player
  • If host fails to reconnect in 90s, remaining players can vote a new host

Spectator Mode

  • Eliminated players become spectators — they see all roles immediately
  • Spectators can see who voted for whom after each round
  • Spectators have a hidden emoji-react toolbar (💀 🙀 😆 👀) visible only to other spectators
  • Observers can join as spectators from the start (no role assigned)
  • Up to 5 spectators per room (free tier), unlimited (premium)
  • Spectator view mode: "Full View" (all roles visible) or "Neutral" (roles hidden)

Authentication & Google SSO

Auth Flow

App Launch → Onboarding Check
If first launch: show 3-step onboarding carousel explaining the game. If returning: check for valid session token. Silent re-auth if token valid.
Sign In Screen
"Continue with Google" button triggers Google One Tap or full OAuth sheet. No email/password option — SSO-only for simplicity and security.
Firebase Auth Processes Token
Google OAuth token passed to Firebase Authentication. Firebase returns UID, email, display name, and profile photo URL.
Profile Creation / Merge
New user: create Firestore profile document. Existing user: load profile, restore preferences, sync stats from Firestore.
Session Token Stored Securely
Firebase ID token stored in an httpOnly cookie (not localStorage — XSS-safe). Refreshed automatically every hour via Firebase SDK. Session stays valid for 30 days of inactivity before re-auth required.

Guest Mode

Players can play without a Google account in Guest Mode. Guests get a randomly generated nickname (e.g., "SnakePlant47") and limited access.

  • Can create or join rooms normally
  • No stats saved between sessions
  • No leaderboard access
  • Can upgrade to full account (Google SSO) without losing current session
  • Guest data merged into Google account on sign-in

What the App Stores

  • Google UID (unique identifier — no password stored)
  • Display name and avatar URL from Google
  • Custom in-game nickname (user-editable, 12 char max)
  • Game stats: games played, wins, eliminations, win rate per role
  • Achievement flags and XP level
  • Purchased word packs and premium status
  • Friends list (mutual follows)
  • App preferences (theme, language, timer default)
Privacy: No personal data sold or shared. Google sign-in used only for identity. Players can delete their account and all data from Settings at any time.

PWA — Why This Matters

A Progressive Web App delivers an app-like experience from the browser — no app store needed. Players on Android can install it to their home screen and it behaves like a native app.

  • Installable on Android and iOS from Chrome/Safari
  • Fullscreen mode — hides browser UI during gameplay
  • Offline fallback: cached UI loads even without internet (game logic still requires connection)
  • Push notifications via Web Push API (Chrome/Android)
  • App icon, splash screen, and orientation lock via Web App Manifest
  • Works in browser too — zero friction for new players

Word Security (Web Context)

  • Words never sent to all clients — each player's word delivered in a private socket event to their socket ID only
  • Words stored in Redis under encrypted keys, expire at game end
  • No word ever appears in URL, query params, or browser history
  • Peek (re-view word) re-requests from server — no local storage
  • Mr. White's payload is identical size to word payloads — traffic analysis-proof
  • HTTPS enforced — all WebSocket connections over WSS

Security & Anti-Cheat

API & Transport Security

  • All HTTP: HTTPS only (TLS 1.3). All WebSocket: WSS only
  • Firebase ID token verified on every socket connection handshake
  • Helmet.js: sets CSP, X-Frame-Options, HSTS, XSS-Protection headers
  • Rate limiting: 100 requests/15min per IP on REST endpoints
  • Socket events rate limited: max 2 events/second per socket
  • CORS whitelist: only approved origins can connect
  • Input sanitized and validated with Zod schemas on every event

Game Integrity

  • Server-authoritative: clients cannot advance game phases themselves
  • All votes validated: player must be alive, must not have voted in this round
  • Role assignment: crypto.randomBytes for cryptographic shuffle
  • Words never broadcast to room — only private socket.emit to specific socketId
  • Game state transitions logged with timestamps — auditable trail
  • Spectators receive redacted game state — roles hidden until revealed

Free Hosting Strategy

The entire stack can run at $0/month using free tiers from four services. The key constraint to design around is cold starts — free servers spin down after inactivity. The architecture below eliminates cold starts as a gameplay problem through smart client-side handling.


🌐

Frontend — Vercel

Free Forever
  • Deploy React/Vite app from GitHub — automatic on every push
  • Global CDN — assets served from the nearest edge node worldwide
  • Free tier: 100GB bandwidth/month — more than sufficient for a game app
  • Custom domain supported on free tier (your own .com)
  • HTTPS automatically provisioned (Let's Encrypt)
  • Preview deployments: every PR gets its own URL for testing
  • Zero configuration needed — Vite auto-detected
Limit to watch: 100GB bandwidth/month. At ~500KB per game session (assets cached after first visit), this supports roughly 200,000 sessions/month before needing an upgrade.
⚡

Backend — Render.com

Free Tier
  • Node.js + Express + Socket.IO server deployed as a Web Service
  • Deploy from GitHub — automatic on push to main branch
  • Free tier: 750 hours/month (enough for 24/7 operation)
  • Supports WebSockets natively — critical for real-time game events
  • Environment variables managed in Render dashboard
  • Automatic HTTPS + WSS — no SSL configuration needed
  • Health check endpoint keeps server awake during active periods
Cold start problem: Free tier spins down after 15 minutes of inactivity. Solution: client-side "warming" — the frontend pings /health on app load. A splash screen animation naturally covers the ~10-second wake time.
🗄️

Database — Supabase

Free Forever
  • PostgreSQL database for word pairs, user profiles, game history
  • Free tier: 500MB storage, 2GB bandwidth/month
  • Supabase Auth as a Firebase Auth alternative (optional: use both)
  • Row Level Security (RLS) — fine-grained access control per user
  • Real-time subscriptions (can replace some Socket.IO use cases)
  • Dashboard with SQL editor, table browser, and migration tooling
  • REST and JS SDK — easy to query from Node.js backend
Word database fit: 2,000 word pairs × ~200 bytes = ~400KB. Comfortably within free tier. Queries cached in Redis so DB is rarely hit during gameplay.
🔴

Cache / State — Upstash Redis

Free Forever
  • Serverless Redis — stores active game room state ephemerally
  • Free tier: 10,000 commands/day, 256MB storage
  • Each active room uses ~2–5KB of Redis storage
  • State auto-expires: rooms set TTL of 24h — no manual cleanup
  • REST API mode: works from serverless environments and Render
  • Used for: active room state, word assignments, vote tallies, clue log
  • Player sessions also cached here — fast reads during gameplay
Capacity math: Each game = ~200 Redis commands. Free tier supports ~50 concurrent games/day. Upgrade to Upstash Pro ($10/month) unlocks unlimited commands.

Auth — Firebase Free Tier

Firebase Auth Limits (Free Spark Plan)

  • 50,000 Monthly Active Users — free forever
  • Google Sign-In included at no cost
  • Unlimited email verification and password reset emails
  • Firebase SDK: modular v10 — only imports what you use (tree-shaken)
  • ID tokens valid for 1 hour, refresh tokens valid indefinitely

Firestore Limits (Free Spark Plan)

  • 1GB storage — stores user profiles, achievements, game history
  • 50,000 reads/day — covers casual player activity well
  • 20,000 writes/day — triggered only on game end, achievement unlock, profile update
  • Firestore reads cached client-side — profile loaded once per session
  • Only write to Firestore at game boundaries, not during real-time play (Redis handles that)

Cold Start Mitigation Strategy

Client-Side Approach

  • On app load, immediately fire a silent GET to GET /health — wakes server in background
  • Splash animation lasts 2–4 seconds — masks the server wake latency completely
  • If server wake takes longer, a "Connecting..." skeleton UI is shown — not a blank screen
  • Socket.IO reconnection: reconnectionAttempts: 10, reconnectionDelay: 1000
  • Toast notification: "Connecting to server..." with spinner if initial connection takes >3s

Server-Side Approach

  • GET /health endpoint returns 200 OK in <5ms — used by both client and Render health checker
  • UptimeRobot (free plan): pings /health every 5 minutes — effectively keeps server awake 24/7 during active periods
  • UptimeRobot limit: free tier monitors 50 URLs every 5 min — more than enough
  • During low-traffic nights (2–6am IST), occasional cold starts are acceptable — no active players
  • Game rooms store "last active" timestamp — stale rooms cleaned up on server restart

Deployment Pipeline

GitHub Monorepo — /client + /server
Single repository with two packages. Client = React/Vite app. Server = Node.js/Express/Socket.IO. Shared types in /shared/types.ts — socket event types, game state interfaces, role enums defined once, used in both client and server.
Push to main → Automatic Deploy
Vercel auto-deploys the client folder. Render auto-deploys the server folder. Both triggered by the same GitHub push. Total deploy time: ~90 seconds frontend, ~2 minutes backend.
Environment Variables — Never in Code
Firebase config in Vercel env vars. Supabase + Redis URLs in Render env vars. Google OAuth client ID in both. No secrets ever committed to git. .env.example documents all required variables for new contributors.
Preview Environments for Testing
Every PR on GitHub gets a Vercel preview URL automatically. Backend can be pointed to a separate Render staging service (or the same one). Test new modes and word packs in preview before merging to main.
Custom Domain Setup
Point undercover.yourdomain.com to Vercel (CNAME record). Point api.undercover.yourdomain.com to Render service. Namecheap and Cloudflare both offer free DNS management.
